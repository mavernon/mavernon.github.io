Note that, whenever the assignment operator is combined with a mathematical operator (e.g. at lines 5 and 6), the mathematical operator is always written first.

Since the do portion of the do while statement (lines 4 thru 6) ensures that any code that immediately follows the do will execute at least once, the value of i after the assignment statement at line 5 completes, is 30. However, the variable i 's value is immediately reassigned (at line 6) as part of logic that occurs just prior to – but in conjunction with the evaluation of the complete while portion of the do while statement.

Because the while loop evaluates to true, this same sequence of actions is repeated again.

After the next iteration of the while loophowever, after the assignment statement at line 5 completes, the value of i is 50; causing the while portion of the do while statement to return false and not execute. The code flow then immediately jumps to the statement at line 7 and executes – outputting the value 50, before the entire program terminates.
